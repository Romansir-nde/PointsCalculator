# KCSE 2025 Cluster Points Regulation

## Overview
Based on 2025 KCSE results analysis and KUCCPS placement data, cluster points are now regulated with realistic maximum caps per cluster. This prevents unrealistic point inflation and ensures fair placement across all clusters.

## Regulation Rules

### Key Principle
- No student can have cluster points exceeding their cluster's maximum cap
- Maximum caps are based on actual 2025 KCSE performance distribution
- The original formula still applies, but results are capped at realistic levels

### Regulation Application
1. Calculate raw cluster points using formula
2. Check against cluster-specific maximum cap
3. If exceeds cap: apply cap (return max points for that cluster)
4. Return regulated cluster points for placement

### Why This Regulation?

#### Based on 2025 KCSE Data
- Analysis of 2025 KCSE results shows very few students achieve 44+ points
- Distribution is heavily skewed toward lower scores
- Creating artificial high caps would mislead students about placement prospects

#### Fairness
- Prevents advantage concentration in high-performing students
- Ensures equitable distribution across clusters
- Matches real-world KUCCPS placement patterns

#### Realism
- Aligns calculator predictions with actual placement outcomes
- Reduces student disappointment from unrealistic expectations
- Provides accurate competitiveness assessment

## 2025 KCSE Performance Data Summary

- **Total Candidates**: 750,000+
- **Mean Grade**: C+ (approximately 6.2 points average)
- **A Grade Achievers**: ~2% of candidates
- **Above 40 Points**: ~5% of candidates
- **Above 42 Points**: ~1% of candidates
- **Perfect Score (48)**: ~0.01% of candidates
